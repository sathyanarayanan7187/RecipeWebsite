NON-VEGETARIAN APPETIZERS

Chicken Malai Tikka is a creamy and mildly spiced appetizer. Boneless chicken pieces are marinated in a mixture of fresh cream, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chili paste, and a blend of spices like garam masala and white pepper. After marinating for a few hours, the chicken is skewered and grilled until tender and juicy. The result is succulent, melt-in-the-mouth chicken with a rich, creamy flavor that‚Äôs enhanced by the smokiness from the grill. Serve with mint chutney and lemon wedges for a delicious start to any meal.

Lamb Seekh Kebabs are spiced minced meat skewers that are popular in Indian cuisine. Ground lamb is mixed with finely chopped onions, green chilies, fresh herbs like cilantro and mint, and spices such as cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The mixture is then molded onto skewers and grilled until charred on the outside yet juicy inside. These kebabs are perfect when paired with a tangy mint chutney or a yogurt dip. The combination of spices and the grilling process give the kebabs a smoky and aromatic flavor that is irresistible.

Fish Amritsari is a crispy and tangy fish appetizer from Punjab. Boneless fish fillets are marinated in a mixture of gram flour, yogurt, carom seeds, red chili powder, turmeric, and a dash of lemon juice. The marinated fish is then deep-fried until golden and crispy. The use of gram flour gives the fish a crunchy coating, while the spices provide a burst of flavor with each bite. This dish is often served with a side of sliced onions and mint chutney, making it a perfect starter for any occasion.

Chicken 65 is a spicy and tangy South Indian appetizer. Chicken pieces are marinated in a mixture of yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, and other spices. After marinating, the chicken is deep-fried until crispy. The fried chicken is then tossed with curry leaves, green chilies, and a splash of lemon juice for added flavor. This dish is known for its vibrant red color and bold, spicy taste, making it a favorite among those who love a bit of heat in their appetizers. Serve hot with a cooling yogurt dip.
