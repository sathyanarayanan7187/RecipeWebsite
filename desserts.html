DESSERTS

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from khoya (reduced milk solids) mixed with a bit of flour and cardamom. The dough is shaped into small balls, deep-fried until golden brown, and then soaked in a warm sugar syrup flavored with rose water or saffron. The syrup is absorbed by the fried balls, making them soft, sweet, and fragrant. Gulab Jamun is often garnished with chopped nuts like almonds or pistachios and is served warm or at room temperature, making it a delightful end to any Indian meal.

Rasmalai is a creamy and luscious dessert made from soft paneer (cottage cheese) balls soaked in thickened, sweetened milk flavored with cardamom and saffron. The paneer balls are first cooked in a light sugar syrup and then transferred to the milk mixture, where they absorb the rich flavors. The dessert is chilled and garnished with slivers of almonds and pistachios before serving. Rasmalai is known for its delicate texture and the rich, aromatic flavor of the saffron-infused milk, making it a favorite among Indian sweets.

Jalebi is a crispy, spiral-shaped dessert soaked in sugar syrup. The batter is made from flour and fermented for a few hours, then piped into hot oil in circular shapes and fried until golden. Once fried, the jalebis are immediately dipped in a warm sugar syrup flavored with saffron and cardamom, which gives them their signature sweetness and a sticky texture. Jalebis are often served hot and are enjoyed during festivals and special occasions. The combination of the crisp exterior and syrupy interior makes Jalebi a beloved treat across India.
